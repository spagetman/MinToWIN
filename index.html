THE FACTS

Form your own opinion:

  • The current federal minimum wage hasn't been raised since 2009. With inflation in mind, the total value of the minimum wage has decreased by about 25% since then.
  • $7.25/hour comes out to a yearly salary of only $3,000 above the poverty line for one person, but if children are added into the equation (even with another working adult) the family's income is always below the federal poverty line.
  • The current wage doesn't meet the cost of living in any state (see graph below). A $15 wage, while not perfect, would close that gap by a large amount.
  • Many people against a higher wage say that the cost of products will increase. However, in states that already enact a $15 wage (New York State, California, D.C.) there has not been a significant change, if at all.
  • In the 1970s-80s, minimum-wage workers needed only 320 hours of labor to pay for state college. Now, people need 4,500.
  • Raising the wage will give the consumer more buying power, thus stimulating the economy.
  • People of all demographics - age, race, and gender - earn the minimum wage; not just younger citizens.

EXPERT OPINION

Take it from us:

The American Dream is known around the world: "if you work hard, you'll rise to the top." In recent years, though, that has been proven wrong solely because of the incredibly low minimum wage. Workers are only paid $7.25 per hour of work, which makes it impossible to support a family. The American Dream has rapidly turned into the American Nightmare. Raising the minimum wage to $15 will not only improve the quality of life for millions of Americans, but it will also stimulate the economy by putting more buying power into the hands of the masses. What would you rather have: corporate greed or families having enough money to raise their children? If you ask me, the choice is an easy one.
